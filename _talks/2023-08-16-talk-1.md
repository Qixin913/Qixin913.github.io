This thesis is based on the dual-carbon goal of carbon peaking and carbon neutrality and the background of the construction of new power systems, and studies the optimal planning of an integrated energy system based on energy storage under the business planning model of energy storage optimisation and photovoltaic farm siting, effective data reduction of regional scenarios, and in-depth research on the path to reduce carbon emissions. The main work is summarised as follows:

1)A literature review of the current state of the field of scenario reduction and energy system optimisation is presented.

2)Scenario reduction using KD algorithm: the original data is 90 sets of 24-hour PV power generation data with a time interval of 15 minutes, and the 90 sets of data are reduced by KD algorithm, and the number of data after reduction is set to 10 sets of data. And the corresponding occurrence probability of the cut data is given.

3)Propose a comprehensive model for energy storage and PV optimisation: for the goal of this paper, consider the minimum carbon emission problem, design the use of NSGA-II algorithm for adaptation, consider the minimum network loss as the objective function for PV siting, and at the same time, consider the constraints on the spending of the dimensions of the energy storage system, such as the operation, installation, maintenance, and the reduction of carbon emissions, and optimise the siting of the storage system and the problem of the fixed capacity.

4)Using the IEEE33 node system as a calculation case: The optimisation synthesis model proposed in this paper is imported into the IEEE33 node system for optimisation calculations, and it can be seen through the calculations that, after importing photovoltaic as well as energy storage, it can reduce up to 2 million yuan of expenditure per year for the energy system. This provides effective engineering optimisation and cost reduction for the energy system, and at the same time reduces the carbon emissions of the system, which indirectly reduces the cost of the energy supply system in terms of carbon emissions.
