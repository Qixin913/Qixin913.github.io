Photovoltaic (PV) inverters play a crucial role in converting the direct current (DC) generated by solar panels into alternating current (AC) suitable for grid connection or local consumption. Traditional PV inverters have relied on the use of transformers for isolation and voltage level adaptation. However, transformer-less PV inverters have gained significant attention in recent years due to their potential for cost reduction, improved efficiency, and smaller size. The basic requirements for PV inverters include average efficiency and electromagnetic interference (EMI) considerations. Average efficiency is a key parameter that determines the effectiveness of energy conversion, impacting the overall performance and economic viability of a PV system. A higher average efficiency implies less energy loss during the conversion process, resulting in increased energy yield and reduced operating costs. EMI, on the other hand, refers to the generation and transmission of unwanted electromagnetic signals during the operation of an inverter. EMI can interfere with other electronic devices, communication systems, and potentially affect the performance of the PV system itself. Mitigating EMI is crucial to ensure proper functioning of the inverter and prevent interference with other electrical systems.
